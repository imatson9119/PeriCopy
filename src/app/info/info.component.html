About Pericopy

The law of the LORD is perfect,

the testimony of the LORD is sure,

As the above passage from Psalm 19:7-8 states, the word of God is perfect, sure, right, and pure, and it is my belief that immersing oneself in it is one of the most beneficial things one can do. Pericopy is a tool that I created to help myself and others memorize scripture more effectively - I hope that you find it useful and that it serves you well as you seek the Lord through His word.

Why Pericopy?


As mentioned above, the primary reason that I created Pericopy was to help myself and others memorize scripture more effectively - I had found that most scripture memorization tools were focused on smaller sections and there were few tools that were designed to help with memorizing larger passages.

Another significant reason I started this project was to help fund friends (and hopefully myself one day) who are pursuing missions in unreached contexts. Currently, your donations go to support my friends who are attending Radius International, a 10-month training program to bring the gospel to unreached language groups. Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get started?
To get started, click the "Test" button in the menu above. You will be prompted to recite a passage of scripture, then you can begin reciting the passage. Once you have finished, click the "Submit" button to see how you did.
How does this work?
When you recite a passage of scripture, Pericopy will compare what you said to the actual text of the passage. From there, it will calculate a score based on how many words you got correct and how many you got wrong. You can then view your score and compare your recitation to the actual text.
Where is my data stored?
Your data is stored locally on your device. Pericopy uses LocalStorage to store your recitation attempts, goals, and other data. This means that your data is not stored on any external servers and is only accessible from the device on which you are using Pericopy. To back up your data or move it to another device, you can use the "Export Data" and "Import Data" buttons under the history page.
